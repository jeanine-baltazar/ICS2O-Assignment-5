University of Toronto

Entrance Requirements

In order to be accepted into the University of Toronto for engineering, students must have completed the Ontario Secondary School Diploma. Applicants must also have completed the following university level courses; English, Calculus and Vectors, Chemistry, Physics, Advanced Functions, one additional university or M level course, proof of english facility. There must be a minimum grade of 70% in the english course. Applicants must meet an average of high 80’s to low 90’s to be eligible for most engineering programs such as chemical engineering, computer engineering, electrical engineering. For the engineering science program, students must have an overall average of low to high 90’s.

Degree/Diploma Requirements

In order to receive a bachelor's degree in engineering at the University of Toronto, students must complete the undergraduate engineering program for their desired field. Completing courses includes receiving 50% or higher in all courses in their undergraduate program. To be eligible to graduate, students must have a weighted session average of at least 60%. Students must also not have gotten more than two marks within the 40%-49% range. Before graduation, engineering students must have also completed a minimum of 600 hours of practical work.

To receive a masters degree in engineering at the University of Toronto, students must complete nine graduate courses and meet the same grade expectations as in the bachelor’s degree. Six of the nine courses must be technical courses and five of the technical courses must be ECE (Electrical & Computer Engineering) courses.

To receive a PhD in engineering, students must maintain an average GPA of A- with no grades below a B-. They must also meet the yearly requirements which include which courses to take, and must do progress review meetings, including the Qualifying Exam.
